🏨 Accommodation Logistics

Where to Sleep on Your 5-Day Journey

Accommodation on the Camino falls into three main categories. For the last 100 km from Sarria, which is the busiest section of the entire route, the logistics of where to sleep depend heavily on whether you want to book in advance or find a bed day-by-day.

Types of Accommodation

Municipal Albergues (Public Hostels) - at least 1 night there for experience

Cost: Very cheap (usually €8-€12 per night).

Logistics: These cannot be booked in advance. Beds are given on a first-come, first-served basis. Pilgrims walking on foot have priority. You must show your Pilgrim's Passport to stay.

Setup: Large dormitory rooms with bunk beds and shared bathroom facilities. Curfews are strictly enforced (usually doors lock at 10:00 PM and you must leave by 8:00 AM).

Private Albergues

Cost: Slightly higher (usually €15-€20 per night).

Logistics: These can be booked in advance, which is highly recommended for this 5-day stretch, especially in the summer months.

Setup: Similar dorm-style setups to municipal albergues, but often with smaller rooms, better facilities, and sometimes the option for a private room.

Pensiones, Hostales, and Casas Rurales (Hotels & Guesthouses)

Cost: Ranging from €40 to €100+ per night depending on the standard.

Logistics: Booked in advance through standard booking sites.

Setup: Private rooms with en-suite bathrooms. Casas Rurales are often located slightly off the main trail, but many offer a pickup and drop-off service for pilgrims.

The Daily Plan

To ensure you have a bed each night on this specific 5-day itinerary, here is how the stops break down:

Night 0 - Sarria

You will need accommodation here the night before you start walking. Since it is a major starting hub, there are dozens of private albergues and hotels.

Night 1 - Portomarín

The town has a large municipal albergue in the center, alongside several private albergues and pensiones.

Night 2 - Palas de Rei

Another major stop with a municipal albergue and numerous private options.

Night 3 - Arzúa

Because the Camino del Norte and Camino Primitivo merge with the Camino Francés before Arzúa, the town gets very crowded. Booking private accommodation here in advance is the safest logistical choice.

Night 4 - O Pedrouzo

This is the final major stop before Santiago. It is a smaller town entirely built around pilgrim services, consisting mostly of albergues and a few pensiones.

Night 5 - Santiago de Compostela

Your destination. There are no municipal pilgrim albergues for multi-night stays here. Most pilgrims book a hotel, apartment, or private hostel near the cathedral to rest.

Booking Strategy

Recommended Approach

For this busy 5-day section:

  • Book your private accommodation in Arzúa in advance (it gets crowded)
  • Book Sarria, Portomarín, and O Pedrouzo if traveling in peak season (June-August)
  • Palas de Rei usually has availability day-of
  • Book Santiago well in advance for celebration nights

Budget considerations: If you mix municipal albergues (€10) with one or two private rooms (€18), you'll average around €12-15 per night, keeping accommodation affordable while ensuring comfort on crowded sections.

What to Know About Albergue Life

  • Check-in: Usually opens at 2:00 PM. Arrive early to secure a good bed.
  • Curfews: Main doors lock at 10:00 PM (some at 9:00 PM). Plan evening meals accordingly.
  • Morning: Beds must be vacated by 8:00 AM (some by 7:00 AM).
  • Shared spaces: Quiet hours start at 9:30 PM. Pilgrims sleep early and wake early.
  • Facilities: Bring your own pillow if picky. Mattresses are usually firm and minimal. Many albergues provide communal kitchens.
  • Community: You'll meet the same pilgrims repeatedly. Dinner is a social event where lasting friendships form.
