💰 Budget Planning

Estimated Total Cost for Your 5-Day Hike

Here is the estimated total budget for your 5-day, 100 km hike from Sarria to Santiago de Compostela. This calculation covers 5 days of walking and 5 nights of accommodation (Sarria, Portomarín, Palas de Rei, Arzúa, and O Pedrouzo). It reflects standard 2026 pricing for the Galicia region.

Note: This budget strictly covers your time on the trail. You will need to calculate your travel to the starting point in Sarria and your final celebratory night of accommodation in Santiago de Compostela separately.

Two Logistical Approaches

To give you an accurate picture, here are two different logistical approaches:

The Minimalist: €180

Carrying your own backpack, staying in municipal albergues, and primarily buying food from supermarkets with occasional cafe stops.

The Comfort Hiker: €330

Using a luggage transfer service, booking beds in private albergues in advance, and eating the Menú del Peregrino at restaurants every night.

Breaking Down Each Expense

🏨 Accommodation

Minimalist: €12/night in municipal albergues (first-come, first-served, no advance booking)

Comfort: €20/night in private albergues (booked in advance, better facilities)

🍽️ Food & Drink

Minimalist: €20/day (early cafe breakfast €4, supermarket lunch €6, budget dinner €10)

Comfort: €35/day (cafe breakfast €5, cafe lunch €12, Menú del Peregrino €18)

🚚 Luggage Transfer

Minimalist: €0 (you carry your backpack)

Comfort: €7/day x 5 days = €35 (Correos or private service)

🧴 Incidentals

Both approaches: €20 for blister treatment, laundry supplies, replacement socks, emergency medication, etc.

Money-Saving Tips

  • Eat the Menú del Peregrino: It's cheaper than ordering à la carte and more filling.
  • Supermarket shopping: Buy lunch items the evening before (bread, cheese, fruit, nuts).
  • Stay in municipal albergues: They're safe, social, and cheap (€8-12 vs €20+ in private).
  • Skip bottled water: Tap water is safe and free. Refill at fountains or ask cafes.
  • Mix approaches: Stay in municipal albergues some nights, private ones on crowded nights (like Arzúa).
  • Picnic lunches: Buying from supermarkets saves €5-10/day compared to cafe lunches.

What's NOT Included in This Budget

  • Transportation to Sarria: Flight, train, or bus to reach the starting point
  • Accommodation in Sarria (Night 0): The night before you start walking
  • Celebration night in Santiago: Most pilgrims stay an extra night to celebrate—budget €40-60 for a nice hotel
  • Initial gear purchases: Backpack, boots, rain gear, sleeping bag (one-time investments)
  • Return transportation: Getting home from Santiago

Final Thoughts on Budget

The Camino is designed to be accessible regardless of budget. The experiences and transformations happen whether you spend €180 or €330. Many pilgrims report that the modest spending actually enhances the spiritual experience—simplicity breeds clarity. Choose the level of comfort that allows you to complete the hike feeling good.
