📍 Your 5-Day Hike

From Sarria to Santiago de Compostela - 115 km Journey kommot track

Certificate Requirements

To officially earn the Compostela when you arrive in Santiago, you must handle two key logistics along the way:

  • The Credential: You must carry a Pilgrim's Passport (Credencial). One can get one in Sarria/Santiago - not a problem
  • The Stamps: You are required to collect at least two stamps (sellos) per day during this final 100 km. You can get these at hostels, cafes, and churches along the route. (in towns where one will stay)

✓ Did you know? Starting in Sarria covers roughly 115 km, which comfortably meets the 100 km minimum requirement to earn your "Compostela" (the official pilgrim certificate).

The 5-Day Itinerary

This is the classic staging for this route, breaking the distance into manageable daily hikes.

Day 1: Sarria to Portomarín

Distance: 22 km (13.6 miles)

Duration: 5 to 6 hours of walking

Description: Your first day involves a steady hike through rural Galicia, crossing small villages and woodland paths, ending with a descent into the town of Portomarín, which sits above a reservoir. Expect about 5 to 6 hours of walking.

Day 2: Portomarín to Palas de Rei

Distance: 25 km (15.5 miles)

Description: This stage features rolling hills and a gradual ascent through the Ligonde mountains. It passes through grassy meadows and stone-built hamlets before arriving in the small town of Palas de Rei.

Day 3: Palas de Rei to Arzúa

Distance: 29 km (18 miles)

Description: This is the longest day of the trek. The route passes through Melide—famous for its pulpo (octopus)—and merges with the Camino Primitivo (the original route) before finishing in Arzúa, known for its local cheese.

Day 4: Arzúa to O Pedrouzo

Distance: 20 km (12.4 miles)

Description: A shorter, relatively flat day primarily walking through eucalyptus forests and quiet country roads as you close the distance to Santiago.

Day 5: O Pedrouzo to Santiago de Compostela

Distance: 20 km (12.4 miles)

Description: The final stretch. You will pass Monte do Gozo (Hill of Joy), where pilgrims traditionally get their first glimpse of the cathedral's spires, before walking the final 5 km into the city center to claim your certificate.

Getting Your Stamps (Sellos)

Remember: You need at least 2 stamps per day to earn your certificate. Stamps are available at:

  • Albergues (hostels) - they will stamp your credential when you check in
  • Cafes and restaurants
  • Churches and chapels
  • Tourist offices in towns

Make sure to ask for your "sello" whenever you stop. Most establishments are happy to provide one for pilgrims.
