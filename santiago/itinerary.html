📍 5-Day Itinerary

Your Day-by-Day Journey

Journey Overview

This carefully planned 5-day itinerary covers approximately 80-85 kilometers along the Camino de Santiago, featuring a mix of challenging mountain passages and picturesque village walks. Each day is designed to be achievable while offering unique experiences and stunning views.

Day 1: The Beginning 🏔️

Route Information

Distance: 15 km

Elevation Gain: 380 m

Time: 5-6 hours

Terrain: Mountain paths and forest trails

Highlights: Mountain views, small villages, pilgrim community first gatherings

What to Expect

Your legs feel fresh but may feel the weight of your backpack. Start with energy but pace yourself—this is a marathon, not a sprint. You'll meet fellow pilgrims and begin settling into the hiking rhythm. The first day sets the tone; be kind to your body and enjoy the journey.

Day 2: The Challenge 💪

Route Information

Distance: 22 km

Elevation Gain: 520 m

Time: 7-8 hours

Terrain: Mountain ascent and forest descents

Highlights: Panoramic views from mountain peaks, dramatic valleys

What to Expect

Day 2 is the longest and most demanding day. Your legs may be sore from Day 1 (welcome to the Camino!). The high elevation gain means slow progress but incredible rewards. Take your time, use trekking poles to save your knees, and celebrate reaching the summit. The views are worth every step.

Day 3: Balance & Breather 🌿

Route Information

Distance: 18 km

Elevation Gain: 380 m

Time: 5.5-6.5 hours

Terrain: Mixed forest and meadow paths

Highlights: Quiet forests, peaceful villages, rolling countryside

What to Expect

A welcome middle day with slightly reduced distance. Your body is adjusting to the daily walking routine. This is often a day for reflection and deeper connections with fellow pilgrims. The scenery is gentle and meditative. Perfect for a slower pace and enjoying the journey rather than just reaching the destination.

Day 4: Strength Returns 🦵

Route Information

Distance: 17 km

Elevation Gain: 410 m

Time: 5-6 hours

Terrain: Mix of forest, farmland, and village paths

Highlights: Rural Spain, traditional villages, warm hospitality

What to Expect

By Day 4, your body has adapted and you'll likely feel stronger. Your hiking legs are strong now. The experience is becoming routine—in a good way. Connect deeply with other walkers who've shared this journey with you. You're in the home stretch; the end is in sight but not yet urgent.

Day 5: The Final Chapter ⭐

Route Information

Distance: 12 km

Elevation Gain: 280 m

Time: 3.5-4.5 hours

Terrain: Final village walk and approach paths

Highlights: Cathedral arrival, emotional completion, pilgrim celebration

What to Expect

The shortest day, but often the most emotional. As you approach the cathedral, you'll likely feel a mix of emotions—accomplishment, gratitude, exhaustion, and perhaps sadness at the journey ending. Take time to absorb the moment. You've done it. You've walked the Camino.

Pro Tips for Your Journey

⏰ Timing

Start walking early (6-7 AM) to avoid afternoon heat. Most pilgrims finish by 1-2 PM. This gives time for laundry, rest, and exploring villages.

💧 Hydration

Drink regularly throughout the day. Don't wait until you're thirsty. Many villages have water fountains. Electrolyte drinks help on hot days.

🥗 Nutrition

Eat substantial meals. Your body is burning 2500+ calories per day. Don't skip breakfast. Pack nuts, fruit, or energy bars for trail snacks.

🧦 Feet Care

This is CRITICAL. Air your feet in accommodations. Treat any blister immediately. Carry specialized blister treatment. Your feet are your best friends—treat them accordingly!
