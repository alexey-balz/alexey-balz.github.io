🎒 Packing & Luggage Logistics

What to Pack and How to Transport It

When organizing your gear for the Camino, you have two primary options for handling your luggage.

Carrying It Yourself

If you prefer the traditional pilgrim approach, you will carry everything on your back.

The Weight Rule

Your fully packed bag should not exceed 10% of your body weight. For most adults, this means a strict limit of 6 to 8 kg (13 to 17 lbs).

The Backpack

A 30L to 40L backpack is the ideal size. Anything larger is unnecessary and will tempt you to overpack.

Packing Essentials

youtube video is a great resource for creating your packing list. Below are the key categories and items to consider:

Because you are hiking day after day, minimalism is required regardless of which transport option you choose.

👕 Clothing

Bring exactly two sets of hiking clothes (one to wear, one to wash in the sink each afternoon), one set of casual evening clothes, and three pairs of merino wool socks to prevent blisters.

🌧️ Weather Protection

A lightweight rain poncho (rain is common in the Galicia region) and a fleece for cool mornings.

😴 Sleeping Gear

A lightweight sleeping bag or a silk sleep liner. Albergues provide a mattress with a disposable cover, but standard bedding is rarely provided.

👟 Footwear

Well-broken-in trail running shoes or lightweight hiking boots, plus a pair of flip-flops or lightweight sandals to wear in the evenings and communal showers.

🩹 First Aid

Sunscreen, ibuprofen, and a dedicated blister kit (hydrocolloid patches like Compeed).

💼 Essentials

Pilgrim credential, toiletries, phone + charger, and any personal medications.

Packing Checklist

Backpack (30L-40L)

  • ☐ Rain poncho or rain jacket
  • ☐ Fleece or light sweater
  • ☐ 2 shirts&t-Shirts
  • ☐ 1 evening shirt
  • ☐ Shorts or pants
  • ☐ 1 pair wool socks just in case
  • ☐ Underwear (2/3 pairs)
  • ☐ Lightweight hiking boots (or crocs)
  • ☐ Flip-flops or sandals
  • ☐ Lightweight sleeping bag or liner
  • ☐ First aid kit + blister patches(!)
  • ☐ Sunscreen
  • ☐ Toiletries (minimal)
  • ☐ Pilgrim credential (see another page for details)
  • ☐ Phone + charger + powerbank
  • ☐ Any required medications

Pro Packing Tips

  • Weigh everything: Bring a small scale or use bathroom scales at home to track total weight.
  • Wash clothes daily: Many albergues have sinks. Hand-washing at night means you'll have dry clothes by morning.
  • Break in boots completely: Do not attempt to break in boots on the Camino.
  • Sporty socks: Such socks are ideal for preventing blisters. They provide excellent breathability andmoisture-wicking properties.
  • Pack light items on top: Put rain gear and clothes on top, heavy items (boots, first aid) at the bottom near your back.
  • Use packing cubes: They save space and keep things organized.
