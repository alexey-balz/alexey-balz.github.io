🎒 Preparation Guide

Get Ready for Your Epic Adventure

Physical Preparation

While the Camino welcomes walkers of all fitness levels, proper training will make your experience more enjoyable. Start training 6-8 weeks before your journey.

Walking Routine

Build up gradually. Week 1-2: 5-10 km walks. Week 3-4: 10-15 km walks. Week 5-6: 15-20 km walks. Week 7-8: 20-25 km walks. Always wear your hiking boots during training to break them in properly.

Strength Training

Focus on legs, core, and lower back. Do squats, lunges, calf raises, and planks 2-3 times per week. Strong legs and a stable core prevent injuries on the trail.

Cardiovascular Fitness

Mix in light jogging, cycling, or swimming 2-3 times per week. Build your cardiovascular endurance to handle daily hiking without excessive fatigue.

Recovery

Don't forget rest days! Your body needs time to recover. Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep nightly and include stretching and foam rolling in your routine.

Essential Gear Checklist

What NOT to Bring

Less is more on the Camino. Avoid these common mistakes:

  • Heavy books or guides: Carry minimal weight. Use apps like Camino.app instead.
  • Multiple pairs of shoes: Break in ONE pair of hiking boots thoroughly before the trip.
  • Too many changes of clothes: Plan to do laundry. Most accommodations offer this service.
  • Heavy camera equipment: Your phone camera is sufficient. Travel light!
  • Hair dryer or unnecessary electronics: Most accommodations have limited power outlets.
  • Fancy clothes or dress shoes: The Camino is casual. Comfort trumps style.
  • Excessive toiletries: You can buy or request items along the route if needed.

Mental Preparation

The Camino is as much a mental journey as a physical one. Here's how to prepare mentally:

  • Set realistic expectations: Days will be hard. Blisters happen. Weather can be unpredictable. Embrace the challenges as part of the experience.
  • Define your personal why: What do you hope to gain from this journey? Personal growth, healing, adventure, spiritual connection?
  • Practice mindfulness: Walking is meditation. Use the journey for self-reflection and inner work.
  • Embrace solitude: While the Camino is social, you'll also have quiet time. Use it for journaling and personal contemplation.
  • Be flexible: Your route might change. You might meet unexpected people. Stay open to surprises—they often become the best memories.
  • Remember your motivation: On difficult days, recall why you started. This will help you push through.
