🥾 Epic Stamps of the Camino

The 10 most unique and memorable passport stamps from Sarria to Santiago de Compostela.

Skip the generic rubber stamps. These stops offer handcrafted art, historical seals, and unique markers representing the true spirit of the final 100km.

1

Trailside Artists

📍 ask locals on the way to Portomarín (Variable)

Independent local artists and musicians often set up small tables along the rural paths before Portomarín. They offer custom, hand-crafted stamps—and sometimes actual hot wax seals (sellos de lacre)—in exchange for a small donation. Ask them for a stamp (in Spanish: "¿Tienes un sello?")

Type: Wax Seal / Handcrafted Art
2

Iglesia de Santiago de Barbadelo

📍 42.7652°, -7.45034°

Located just a few kilometers outside of Sarria, this 12th-century Romanesque church offers an intricate stamp reflecting its historic architecture. Getting it requires timing your walk to pass by when the doors are open.

Type: Historical Ink Stamp
3

The 100km Milestone Cafés (Ferreiros / Brea area)

📍 ~42.82, -7.63 (check "All camino" app to be sure where it is)

You cannot stamp your passport on the famous "100 km" stone marker itself, but the small cafés and vendors positioned immediately around this exact boundary in Ferreiros / Brea offer unique stamps specifically commemorating crossing this crucial threshold.

Type: Commemorative Threshold Stamp
4

Oficina Rural de Correos (Portomarín)

📍 42.8075°, -7.61666°

Instead of a standard rubber stamp, the Spanish Post Office offers an official philatelic tourist postmark (matasellos). This legally dated ink cancellation features local monuments, resembling an official passport visa.

Type: Official Postmark (Matasellos)
5

Iglesia de San Salvador de Vilar de Donas

📍 42.8926°, -7.80974°

Requires a 2-kilometer detour off the main path near Palas de Rei. It is a 12th-century national monument containing ancient frescoes and the tombs of the Knights of the Order of Santiago. The caretakers are deeply knowledgeable.

Type: Order of Santiago Stamp
6

Octopus Ezequiel (Melide)

📍 42.91347°, -8.01254°

Melide is famous for pulpo a la gallega. Getting your passport stamped at this massive, chaotic pulpería proves you took part in the Camino's most famous communal eating tradition.

Type: Culinary / Restaurant Stamp
7

Albergue de Ribadixo

📍 42.93076°, -8.13056°

Located immediately after crossing a medieval bridge over the River Iso, this is a 15th-century pilgrim hospital that has continuously operated for centuries. It connects your credencial directly to medieval hospitality.

Type: Historical Hospital Stamp
8

Ermita de Santa Irene

📍 42.917°, -8.3307°

Hidden in dense eucalyptus forests, this small chapel dedicated to a Portuguese martyr is easy to walk right past. Featuring a nearby "fountain of eternal youth," it offers a classic, hard-earned stamp in a tranquil setting. Its open only on 29.6 and so one can get stamp there only on that day. Otherwise go to the adjacent albergues (they keep the stamp)

Type: Secluded Chapel Stamp
9

Lavacolla River Area

📍 42.9005°, -8.449°

Historically, this is where medieval pilgrims would strip down and wash themselves in the river to be clean before entering Santiago. Getting a stamp from a local parish or business here honors this ancient purification tradition.

Type: Historical Tradition Stamp
10

The Sacristy of the Cathedral

📍 42.8806°, -8.5443°

While the Pilgrim's Reception Office gives the final stamp for your Compostela, the ultimate secret stamp is found inside the Cathedral itself. Visit the Sacristy to ask the clergy for the official internal Cathedral stamp.

Type: Exclusive Clergy Stamp
